### [BODY COPY]

The UK-designed Maverick guitars, built in Korea and set up and QC'd here, feature a number of smart innovations. From the unique Evolution roller tone and volume controls to the sharply contoured body styling, the F-1 is full of nice surprises. For a guitar company still in its infancy, Maverick have matched smart design and construction with excellent marketing – the British Ibanez?

A few important modifications have been made in response to suggestions after the guitar's first encounter with the UK guitar press. The effective travel of the Evolution control pots has been limited to a single sweep, taking you from zero to 10, or vice versa, in a single movement as opposed to the DJ-style digital dexterity that was necessary before. The pickups have also been modified, with the help of Trevor Wilkinson who was involved in the initial design of the instruments. These direct-mounted humbuckers were voiced here in the UK, then reproduced in Korea… but more about them a little later.

Maverick proclaim their three-piece maple/bubinga/maple laminate neck construction to be one of the thinnest production necks available, and with a very similar dimension to Ibanez's Wizard neck, this is very true. But thin needn't mean uncomfortable – the rounded edges of the rosewood fingerboard and the 24 hand-polished medium jumbo frets along with the shallow 'C' profile and satin finish all help to create a highly hospitable playing surface. In short, the F-1 is an utter treat to play and positively encourages wide rock vibrato and smooth bending.

Maverick's vibrato system owes more to Ibanez's Edge version than the original Floyd Rose. A push-fit vibrato arm mounted in a tension-adjustable nylon collar is another player-friendly detail, as well as the snazzy rubber O-rings on the upgraded Japanese toggle switch and vibrato arm. When strapped on, the commodious woody tone, reminiscent of DiMarzio's Super Distortion model. We were completely bowled over by the quality of tone and sustain on offer. The neck unit is like dark chocolate – smooth and sweet but with that bitter edge for extra clarity. The bridge humbucker, on the other hand, is a metal monster. With heaps of gain, the sound is crisp and chugging at the same time. It's perfect for staccato riffs and biting leadwork. Unusually for this type of guitar, the clean styles on offer from both pickups are exceptional. Never muddy or characterless and they ring out with a bright punchy hi-fi tone.

### [SECTION HEADER]

**Verdict**

Maverick is doing a great job at providing a viable alternative to the more established rock-axe brands. The build quality and sound of this sample is impressive as is the UK-tweaked playability – you're guaranteed a pro set-up on your new guitar. Consequently, we really enjoyed playing the F-1 – it's a high-quality rock guitar with a great neck and frets, cool features and sensible price.

The only small gripe we have is the lack of coil-splits for the humbuckers. The Evolution design doesn't yet support this option so save for installing a mini-toggle switch, or a five-way selector, we'll have to wait for greater sonic versatility. Having said that, the three sounds on offer are very good indeed.

All in all, we'd like to bet you're going to be hearing a lot more from Maverick. Quality matters.
