### Key Notes

| Note | Detail |
|---|---|
| **Joint registration** | Both founders named — unlike 2096867 (Stock alone). Suggests joint ownership agreement on this specific design. |
| **Expiry timing** | Expired 4 March 2007 — NOT renewed. Maverick announced cessation of trading September 2007. Failure to renew this design is consistent with company winding down. |
| **5-year duration** | UK registered designs at this time required renewal every 5 years for up to 25 years maximum. Non-renewal at first opportunity (2007) strongly suggests financial difficulty or deliberate wind-down. |
| **Design filed 2002** | Filed March 2002 — consistent with 2002 catalogue featuring the MatriX neck on X-Treme and Matrix models. The Guitarist Winter 2001 review noted the MatriX fingerboard as a unique feature. |
| **What the design protects** | The distinctive diagonal split between two contrasting fingerboard wood species (rosewood/maple) — described in the 2002 catalogue as "only seen on a small number of one-off custom guitars" prior to Maverick's production use |
| **Rear view significance** | Sheet 5 rear view reveals the bubinga reinforcement strip — confirming this structural/visual element was part of the protected design |
| **Cross-references** | Confirmed in 2002 catalogue and 2004 catalogue small print as design No. 3001705. Named 'Maverick Matrix Guitar Neck' in all catalogue legal text. |
| **Gen 2 note** | The MatriX neck design was dropped from the 2004 catalogue range — the non-renewal of this design in 2007 is consistent with the design no longer being in active production use. |
