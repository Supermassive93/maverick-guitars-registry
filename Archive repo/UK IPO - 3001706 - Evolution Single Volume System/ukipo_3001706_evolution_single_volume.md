### Key Notes

| Note | Detail |
|---|---|
| **Distinguishes from 2096867** | Design 2096867 protects the dual V&T roller system. This design (3001706) separately protects the single-roller volume-only variant used on D-Tox series |
| **Batch filing with 3001705** | Both 3001705 and 3001706 were filed 4 March 2002, granted 9 July 2002, expired 4 March 2007 — filed together as a batch |
| **Joint registration** | Both founders named — same joint ownership structure as 3001705 |
| **Expiry timing** | Expired 4 March 2007 — same day as 3001705, NOT renewed. Six months before Maverick's September 2007 closure announcement. Non-renewal of both designs on the same date strongly indicates deliberate wind-down decision. |
| **D-Tox identification** | The guitar shown in application images is identifiable as the D-Tox model — X-body or F-body with single volume Evolution control, as described in the 2002 catalogue D-Tox pages |
| **Cross-references** | Confirmed in 2002 catalogue and 2004 catalogue small print as design No. 3001706. Named 'Maverick Evolution Single Volume System' in all catalogue legal text. |

### Analytical Notes — IP Portfolio

| Observation | Significance |
|---|---|
| Design 2096867 registered to Stock alone | Personal IP ownership — not held by company |
| Designs 3001705 and 3001706 joint Stock/James | Suggests a shift to shared ownership for 2002 filings |
| 3001705 and 3001706 both lapsed March 2007 | Non-renewal on same date = deliberate decision, not oversight |
| March 2007 lapse precedes September 2007 closure by 6 months | Company likely knew in early 2007 it would not continue trading |
| 2096867 ran full 10 years to October 2010 | Either renewed by Stock personally post-closure, or ran its original maximum term without needing renewal under earlier design law |
| MatriX neck design (3001705) dropped from 2004 catalogue | IP protection allowed to lapse on designs no longer in production |
| All three designs now expired | No active IP protection on any Maverick design element as of 2026 |
