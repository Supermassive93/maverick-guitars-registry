"WHEN WE STARTED IN 1999 WE WERE DETERMINED TO STAY TRUE TO OURSELVES… MAVERICK WAS NEVER ABOUT THE MONEY"

---

### [ARTICLE BODY COPY]

Maverick Guitars, the UK-based company behind the popular X-Series, F-Series and Species rock guitars, has announced that it will cease trading. However, according to the company's director Mark James, this may not be the end of the Maverick story.

"As soon as the time is right and I feel we have something valid to say, Maverick will be back," said Mark in an exclusive interview with *Total Guitar*. "We have always been passionate to the point of self-destruction and have never wanted our guitars to be just another product on the shelf. If that means taking a step away from the market for a while, then I believe that is the best thing for Maverick to do."

In an industry that was dominated by America and the Far East, Maverick showed that the Brits could come up with uniquely designed, quality electric guitars at a competitive price. "When we started in 1999 we were determined to be true to ourselves," continues Mark. "If that meant we sold a lot of guitars, then great. If it meant we sold only a few to our fellow lunatics around the world then that was fine too. Maverick was never about the money."

As the hardest-working man in the guitar industry prepares to bow out, Mark James looks back with pride on what he has achieved with Maverick Guitars. "We built some brilliant guitars, had a fantastic rollercoaster ride and gained many real Maverick fans. Plus, I've watched artists playing our guitars onstage in front of thousands of people. Believe me, that's an amazing feeling!"

Maverick owners can get info on customer support from Maverick's website (www.maverickguitars.com). TG offers Mark James our best wishes and looks forward to the return of Maverick Guitars. ■

### STORY 1 — Brits design metal axe

#### [HEADLINE]

**Brits design metal axe**

#### [IMAGE CAPTION 1 — Below close-up photo of controls]

Dig the maverick's groovy roller volume and tone controls, baby!

#### [IMAGE CAPTION 2 — Top right, beside guitar photos]

The Brits rock out, with a nod towards BC Rich and Ibanez

#### [BODY COPY]

The UK isn't renowned for its home-grown rock and metal axes but Maverick Guitars look set to change that with a new range. Designed in the UK in association with Trev Wilkinson, and manufactured in Korea, the guitars feature unique and patented volume and tone roller controls mounted on the side of the instruments. They are also offering what they believe are the thinnest and fastest production necks of any brand. The 24-fret neck is made of maple laminated with bubinga and it features a dual-action truss rod. The bodies are made of basswood.

As you'd expect, the F1 and X1 guitars, which will both retail at £499, feature a low profile double-locking Floyd Rose licensed vibrato and they're joined by the B1 bass (£469). We can look forward to an exotic wood, oil-finished version of the F1 early next year. Rock on!

Contact: Maverick Guitars 01454-238850

### STORY 2 — Fylde expand mandolin family

#### [HEADLINE]

**Fylde expand mandolin family**

#### [IMAGE CAPTION — Beside mandolin photos]

Fylde instruments get real names, like Touchstone. Good!

#### [BODY COPY]

North-west maker Fylde Guitars reckon that with the addition of their new Touchstone mandolin and mandola, they have the 'most extensive range of its kind in the world'. Their mandolin family range boasts 16 instruments and they make nine different acoustic guitar models.

The Touchstone models are quite a departure in shape and sound for Fylde, who have been making mandolins and mandolas for 20 years. Both models feature sapele back and sides, spruce tops and ebony fingerboards. The Touchstone mandolin is larger than Fylde's existing model – now called the Octavius – making it easier to play thanks to the longer scale length and greater fingerboard space. The larger soundbox means that it produces a bigger, richer sound. The Touchstone mandola adheres to a similar concept and is designed for the 'proper' mandola tuning of CGDA.

The Touchstone mandolin retails at £520, the Touchstone mandola at £630.

Contact: Fylde Guitars 01768-891515  
E-mail: rogerbucknall@fyldeguitars.com

### STORY 3 — Not so wacko Jacko…

#### [HEADLINE]

**Not so wacko Jacko…**

#### [BODY COPY]

Are you a Jackson or Charvel player? Want to hear news and views about products and players? Well, Ian Neill and Neil Chapman want to find out if there's enough interest from users of these fine guitars to start a club, along the lines of the Fender Club and as a strictly non-profit making organisation. "The idea", say Ian and Neil, "is to publish a regular newsletter, containing information of a specific interest to Charvel and Jackson users, at all playing and price levels, and to become a forum for users of the guitars to share information, trade bits and pieces and to generally get fans of these instruments in touch with one another." So, if you're interested and fancy adding your name to the list, call Ian Neill on 01749 342935, or Neil Chapman on 0771 4364032
